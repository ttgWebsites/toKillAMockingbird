Essay Topics

  • Discuss the trial, and how it ties into the novel.
  • The trial of Tom Robinson seems to encapsulate the whole theme of the novel.
  • In the novel the idea of innocence and its place in a world divided by good and evil plays a prevalent role.
  • Scout and Jem are still young children when the novel begins, and slowly, over time, they are forced to mature and are introduced to the reality of the adult world.
  • This process is greatly sped up by the events of the Robinson trial.
  • They are witness to a terrible injustice due to a man's skin color and come to realize that outside their home, life can be a very dark experience.
  • The trial is the central piece of the novel, and in a few short chapters shows the readers how quickly a person can mature due to life experience.
  • It is the events at the trial that seem to alter the children's prospective on life.
  • Talk about Atticus as a father and his style of parenting.
  • As a single father, Atticus must balance raising his children as well as providing for them.
  • He is therefore forced to hire help around the house when he cannot be home, however this does not detract from his parenting.
  • Atticus is a central figure in his children's lives and works hard to raise them as respectful and virtuous individuals.
  • Atticus shows that he can be strict, however much of this is for Scout and Jem's own good, as he desires them to be the best possible children.
  • It is clear that he does not share many of the southern views towards blacks and he makes it very clear to his children that everyone is created equal and prejudices should never exist.
  • Overall he proves to be a wonderful and well-rounded parent.
